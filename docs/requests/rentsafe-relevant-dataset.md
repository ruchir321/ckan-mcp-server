Here are the datasets formatted in a table with direct links to their Toronto Open Data portal pages and a short description of their relevance when overlaid with RentSafeTO data:

| Dataset Name | Link to Dataset | Relevance / Overlay Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **311 Service Requests (Customer Initiated)** | [View Dataset](https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/311-service-requests-customer-initiated) | Maps the real-time, daily lived experiences of tenants (e.g., pest, heating, or noise complaints) to verify if official RentSafeTO audit scores align with tenant reality. |
| **Municipal Licensing and Standards (ML&S) - Investigation Activity** | [View Dataset](https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/municipal-licensing-and-standards-investigation-activity) | Highlights "chronic offenders" by tracking actual enforcement actions, bridging the gap between a low-scoring building and the specific property standards landlords are legally investigated for. |
| **Short-Term Rentals Registration** | [View Dataset](https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/short-term-rentals-registration) | Identifies "ghost hotels." A high concentration of short-term rentals often strains building amenities; overlaying this reveals if transient guests correlate with rapid drops in building security or cleanliness scores. |
| **Subsidized Housing Listings** | [View Dataset](https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/subsidized-housing-listings) | Facilitates equity analysis by comparing the evaluation scores of private/market-rate apartments against subsidized (Rent-Geared-to-Income) buildings to assess living standards for vulnerable populations. |
| **Toronto Community Housing (TCHC) Data** | [View Dataset](https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/toronto-community-housing-data) | Maps public housing developments, allowing for a direct comparison of maintenance and evaluation scores between city-run public housing and private market landlords. |
| **Neighbourhood Profiles** | [View Dataset](https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/neighbourhood-profiles) | Aggregates census data (income, ethnicity, immigration status). Spatially joining this with RentSafe scores reveals whether systemic inequalities exist, such as lower maintenance standards in lower-income areas. |
| **Basement Flooding by Ward** | [View Dataset](https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/basement-flooding-by-ward) | Tracks severe weather impact and sanitary/sewer blockages. Essential for identifying if low-scoring structures logically correlate with systemic plumbing or flood-prone vulnerabilities. |
